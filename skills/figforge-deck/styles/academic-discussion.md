# Style: academic-discussion (学术讨论)

Target: a seminar / lab meeting / journal-club / conference talk slide. Restrained,
evidence-first, low-decoration. Reads as serious scholarship rather than corporate pitch.

---

## Anchors (constant across the deck — never vary mid-deck)

1. **Color palette (3 colors, fixed)**
   - base: deep navy `#1B2A41`
   - secondary: warm off-white `#F6F2E7`
   - accent: rust `#B8442C`

2. **Typography**
   - display: a transitional or old-style serif (think Source Serif Pro / Charter / Crimson)
   - body: a humanist sans (think Inter / Source Sans / Lato)
   - never use display script, brush, or display-decorative fonts

3. **Layout grid**
   - cover / closing → centered hero
   - section → centered numeral over title
   - content → ⅓-⅔ asymmetric: title block left, single restrained schematic right

4. **Recurring motif**
   - a thin (1–2 px equivalent) horizontal rule under the slide number, top-left corner,
     on every slide of the deck — same length, same position

5. **Background type**
   - warm off-white solid for cover / content / closing
   - section pages may invert to deep navy with off-white text

---

## Per-role layout

### cover

- Background: warm off-white solid
- Title: large serif, centered, deep-navy color, generous whitespace above and below
- Subtitle (≤1 caption): small humanist sans, centered, ~2 lines below the title
- Top-left motif: tiny serif "00" + thin rust hairline beneath it
- Nothing else on the slide

### section

- Background: deep navy solid (inverted)
- Numeral: very large serif "01" / "02" / ... in warm off-white, top-left aligned
- Title: serif, warm off-white, on the same baseline as the numeral or just below it
- Optional 1-line caption: small humanist sans in muted off-white, beneath the title
- Single rust hairline beneath the numeral as motif (slightly heavier than other slides)

### content

- Background: warm off-white solid
- Top-left motif as defined (number + thin rule)
- Left ⅓ vertically centered: serif title (deep navy) on top, captions (humanist sans, smaller) below
- Right ⅔: ONE restrained schematic visual (line drawing, abstract diagram, simple chart silhouette)
  in deep-navy ink on warm off-white. The visual MUST relate to the slide's title concept.
- No bullet rows, no icon grids

### closing

- Background: warm off-white solid
- Centered serif word: "Discussion" / "Thanks" / "Q & A" / "References" — pick one
- Optional 1-line caption beneath, small humanist sans, deep navy, muted

---

## Visual element voice (the right ⅔ of content slides)

- Prefer monochrome line drawings, schematic glyphs, abstract geometric forms
- Prefer sparse, ink-on-paper feel
- Avoid: photo-realistic imagery, gradients, drop shadows, fake holograms, glow,
  rainbow palettes, brand logos, real-person faces
- The visual is illustrative, not literal. A "diffusion" slide does not need a real
  diffusion equation; it needs a schematic that evokes diffusion (gradient cells fading,
  abstract particle scatter)

---

## What NOT to do in this style

- No bullet points
- No call-out boxes / pills / chips
- No emoji
- No ribbon banners
- No glossy buttons
- No three-icon-grid summary slide
- No accent line directly under the title (the canonical AI-slide tell)
