# Style: editorial-creative (设计 / 媒体 / 创意展示)

Target: a design talk, magazine-style cover, creative portfolio walkthrough. Bold
typography + asymmetric layout + confident use of negative space.

---

## Anchors (constant across the deck)

1. **Color palette**
   - base: bone `#F2EDE3`
   - secondary: char `#181818`
   - accent: vermilion `#E0432C`

2. **Typography**
   - display: high-contrast serif or wide grotesk (think Playfair Display / NeueHaas
     Display / GT Sectra)
   - body: a clean sans (think Inter / Söhne)
   - prefer the display VERY large; the body remains small and quiet

3. **Layout grid**
   - cover → asymmetric oversized display title
   - section → vermilion full-bleed with reversed colors
   - content → asymmetric: title spans left ⅔ giant, captions stack right ⅓ small
   - closing → centered display phrase

4. **Recurring motif**
   - a fat vermilion bar (≈ 8–12 % of the slide width) anchored to ONE side of every
     slide. Alternate sides between consecutive slides for rhythm (left, right, left, right…)

5. **Background type**
   - bone for cover / content / closing
   - vermilion full-bleed for section pages (the only place vermilion dominates)

---

## Per-role layout

### cover

- Background: bone
- Vermilion bar anchored to the left edge (full slide height)
- Title: oversized display serif in char, may extend close to the safe area for drama
  (but never crop — keep the 8 % safe margin)
- Subtitle (≤1 caption): small sans in char, in a quiet position (often just under the
  title or in the bottom-right)

### section

- Background: vermilion full-bleed
- Numeral: very large display serif "01" / "02" in char, top-left
- Section title: same display serif in char, beneath the numeral
- Bone bar anchored to the right edge (the inversion: bone bar on vermilion bg)

### content

- Background: bone
- Vermilion bar anchored to alternating side (left for odd slides, right for even slides)
- Title: giant display type in char, spans left ⅔ of the slide
- Captions (≤2): small sans in char, stacked in the right ⅓
- Optional: a single bold abstract editorial visual sits behind the title in low-opacity
  vermilion line — large geometric letterform, abstract shape, or editorial line drawing

### closing

- Background: bone
- Single centered display phrase in char: "End." / "Fin." / "More?" / "Thanks."
- Vermilion bar anchored to the bottom edge (full slide width, the reversal: a bar that
  visually closes the deck)

---

## Visual element voice

- Prefer bold abstract shapes, editorial line drawings, oversized graphic letters used
  as visual anchors, magazine-style negative space
- Prefer big-and-quiet: one giant element per slide, surrounded by deliberate emptiness
- Avoid: corporate "team handshake", stock photography, generic icon sets, three-card
  grids, gradient backdrops

---

## What NOT to do

- No three-up icon grids
- No bullet lists
- No gradients
- No drop shadows on text
- No accent line directly under the title (the vermilion bar IS the accent; nothing
  else accents the slide)
- No emoji
- No symmetric, balanced compositions — this style lives by asymmetry
